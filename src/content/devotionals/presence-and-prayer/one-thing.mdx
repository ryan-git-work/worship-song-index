"One thing have I asked of Yah."

Not ten things.
Not everything at once.
One thing.

There is a kind of clarity that comes when you stop trying to carry the whole world and you simply seek God.

Presence and prayer are not add ons. They are the foundation. If you lead worship without presence, you will eventually start leading from mechanics. It might sound good, but it will feel thin.

This verse is not about wanting a moment. It is about wanting God.

To behold His beauty.
To inquire in His temple.
To stay near.

Before you lead anyone else, choose the one thing.

Sit with God.
Breathe.
Let your soul quiet.
Then step into rehearsal with a heart that is already turned toward Him.
